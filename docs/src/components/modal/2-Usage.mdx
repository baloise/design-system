## When to Use

- To interrupt the user with a decision or action that must be completed before continuing.
- To confirm a destructive or irreversible action (e.g. delete, submit).
- To display a focused form or secondary workflow without navigating away from the current page.
- To surface critical information that requires acknowledgement.

## When NOT to Use

- For non-critical or informational messages — use a notification or snackbar instead.
- When the content is too large to fit comfortably in a dialog — consider a dedicated page or side panel.
- For complex multi-step workflows — break them into separate pages instead.
- As a substitute for inline validation or contextual help.
